Objective:
Redesign the Schedule page of The Ledger into an Operational Control Layer that overlays intelligence onto existing QuickBooks-connected data without modifying or duplicating accounting functionality.

Core Rules

The Ledger is connected to the QuickBooks API.

All financial data must be READ-ONLY.

Do NOT recreate accounting reports (no P&L, no balance sheet, no expense ledger views).

Do NOT write financial data back to QuickBooks.

Only compute forward-looking operational insights based on scheduled data + QuickBooks values.

🧱 1. Page Layout Redesign

Keep the left sidebar navigation unchanged.

Replace the current empty calendar grid layout with:

A. Top Summary Bar (Weekly Intelligence Strip)

Add a horizontal metrics bar above the calendar grid containing:

Workforce Utilization %

Equipment Utilization %

Total Scheduled Revenue (week)

Forecasted Labor Cost (week)

Net Forecast Contribution (week)

Overtime Risk Indicator (if > 85% capacity)

These are forward-looking projections only.

B. Calendar Grid (Week View Default)

Maintain week view structure but redesign job tiles:

Each job card must display:

Job Name

Client Name

Contract Value (read-only from QB)

Invoiced-to-Date %

Scheduled Labor Hours (that day)

Crew Count

Margin Status Indicator (Green / Yellow / Red)

Color coding:

Green: Healthy projected margin

Yellow: Margin compression risk

Red: Low/negative projected margin

Margin status is computed internally using:
(Projected Revenue - Estimated Total Cost) / Projected Revenue

Do NOT store this value in QuickBooks.

C. Right-Side Operational Drawer (Dynamic Panel)

When a day or job is clicked, open a right-side drawer showing:

For Selected Job:

Contract Value (read-only)

Cost to Date (read-only)

Scheduled Remaining Labor

Assigned Crew Breakdown

Equipment Assigned

Upcoming Invoice Milestone (if applicable)

Internal Forecasted Margin %

Risk Flags (if any)

For Selected Day:

Total Scheduled Revenue

Total Estimated Labor Cost

Crew Allocation %

Equipment Allocation %

Net Forecast Contribution (daily)

This panel must be dynamic and not redirect away from the page.

⚙️ 2. Operational Intelligence Layer

Implement:

Resource Conflict Detection

Flag double-booked crew

Flag equipment conflicts

Flag overtime risk (>8 hours/day or >40 hours/week per worker)

Display warning icons inside job cards.

Capacity Awareness

At week level:

Total available crew hours

Total scheduled crew hours

Capacity Remaining %

At equipment level:

Utilization per equipment category

Smart Filters (Top Right)

Add dropdown filter options:

View by Margin Status

View by Crew

View by Equipment

Show Only High-Risk Jobs

Show Underutilized Days

View by Client

Filtering should dynamically update the grid.

🧮 3. Data Architecture Requirements

Do NOT duplicate accounting tables.

Create internal models for:

Scheduled Labor Hours

Crew Assignments

Equipment Assignments

Internal Labor Rates

Internal Equipment Rates

Forecast Calculation Engine

Pull from QuickBooks API:

Invoice totals

Estimate values

Cost-to-date

Expense totals

Payroll cost data (if available)

All QuickBooks data must be cached locally but treated as read-only.

📊 4. Forecast Logic Layer

Create a calculation service that:

For each job:

Reads contract value from QB

Reads cost-to-date from QB

Adds projected remaining labor cost

Adds projected equipment cost

Outputs forecasted margin %

For each day:

Sums scheduled job revenue portions

Sums projected daily labor cost

Outputs projected daily contribution

No financial records are written back.

🎨 5. UI Improvements

Compact job tiles (no large empty whitespace)

Color-coded margin health

Small icon indicators for warnings

Smooth drawer slide animation

Responsive week/month toggle

Sticky top intelligence bar

🚫 Explicitly Exclude

No general ledger entries

No tax reporting

No reconciliation tools

No expense categorization page

No accounting-style reports

This page must remain operational, not financial-reporting.

🎯 Final Outcome

The Schedule page becomes:

A profit-aware operational planning system that enhances scheduling decisions using QuickBooks-connected financial context without replacing accounting.
